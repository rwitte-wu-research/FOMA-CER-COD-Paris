## Purpose

Block A of the locked battery [DEC-031]: establish the headline pooled CER–COD association and its credibility envelope — variance decomposition, prediction intervals overall and per Paris period, working-correlation sensitivity, estimator robustness, and the economic translation [DEC-012/012a] — as the anchor for all subsequent blocks.

## Methods

Three-level meta-analysis with robust variance estimation on Fisher-z partial correlations (van Aert 2023; v_i = 1/(n_eff − 3)): `rma.mv` with `~1 | cluster_id/study/esid`, CHE working covariance with ρ = 0.6 imputed within cluster_id [F56: the one multi-study cluster exists because of sample overlap], CR2 small-sample robust inference with Satterthwaite df clustered on cluster_id; back-transformation to r for reporting only. Prediction intervals use the CR2-consistent construction with all three variance components [F55; plan Addendum A.8]. Eight ES from two studies have unrecoverable sample windows and drop listwise from period cells only [DEC-042b]. Estimator robustness: one-effect-per-cluster (CS ρ = 0.6 aggregation, REML + Knapp–Hartung), UWLS+3 (Stanley et al. 2024, PCC metric, CR2 on cluster_id), Hunter–Schmidt bare-bones and WAAP (Stanley et al. 2017) at ES level as appendix benchmarks [F54]. ρ ∈ {0.4, 0.8} sensitivity per E1.

## Results

**Headline (A1).** r = −0.059, 95% CI [−0.086; −0.031], df = 111.7, p < 0.0001. Small, precisely estimated, negative.

**Heterogeneity (A2/A3).** 95% PI [−0.390; +0.287] — sign-inclusive; a single new study can plausibly show either direction. Variance shares: 62.3% between clusters, ≈0% study-within-cluster (boundary; only one multi-study cluster exists), 37.5% within-study/ES level, 0.14% sampling.

**Paris periods (A3, descriptive; the formal test is B1).** Pre: r = −0.060 [−0.098; −0.022], 1,994 ES / 83 studies, df 80.3, PI [−0.440; +0.338]. Post: r = −0.049 [−0.066; −0.031], 711 ES / 31 studies, df 27.5, PI [−0.208; +0.113]. The point contrast is small (+0.011 toward zero post-Paris) and untested here by design [DEC-031a.6].

**Sensitivity and robustness (A4/A5).** ρ = 0.4 / 0.8: r = −0.059 / −0.058 — invariant. One-effect-per-cluster: −0.062 [−0.109; −0.013], KnHa df 113. UWLS+3: −0.031 [−0.087; +0.025], CR2 df = 2.28 — disclosed [F57]: the top cluster carries 37.9% of the precision weight (top two: 52.6%); CI unreliable below df 4 (Tipton), point estimate same sign and order of magnitude. HS bare-bones: −0.022 [−0.024; −0.019]; SE anti-conservative under dependence (k = 2,713 ES), appendix benchmark. WAAP: only 704 of 2,713 ES (49 clusters) are adequately powered against the pooled effect; WAAP = −0.031 [−0.093; +0.031], df 2.22 disclosed (top cluster 41.1% / top-2 56.9% of powered-subset weight). The 74% underpowered share feeds the power narrative (Ioannidis et al. 2017).

**Economic translation (A6; constants PENDING DEC-012a).** Under placeholder SD(COD) ∈ {100, 150, 200} bp: Δ COD per 1-SD CER improvement = −5.9 / −8.8 / −11.7 bp. Constant-free anchor: |r| = 0.84 × the Doucouliagos (2011) "small" benchmark (0.07). ## Verdict

The pooled CER–COD association is a small, precisely estimated negative effect that sits below the field's own "small" benchmark and translates to single-digit basis points under plausible scale anchors — economically modest, exactly as the paper's framing requires. It is robust in sign and order of magnitude across working correlations and estimator families; the FE-type UWLS+3 halves it because two mega-sample clusters dominate the precision weights, which is itself the documented rationale for the hierarchical headline. Heterogeneity is the dominant feature of the evidence base: the sign-inclusive prediction interval, not the mean, carries the substantive story. The descriptive pre/post-Paris contrast is negligible at the pooled level — consistent with the expected approximately-null Paris result — and its formal test belongs to B1.
